  • Rhythm Games.
    Wanna know what Rhythm Games are? Click here and read for yourself! As the name suggests, "Rhythm Games" is a genre of games, where the main goal is to interact along the rhythm of the level. All rhythm games have songs, and the interactions in the game must be done with the beat of the songs. There are usually 4 difficulty levels: Easy, Normal, Hard, and Expert. As the difficulty increases, the number of notes per second increases.

    TL;DR: Tap the approaching notes to the rhythm of the song.
    The player must have extraordinary reflexes and sense of rhythm to perfectly clear Expert level rhythm games.

  • Light Novels.
    Novels specifically from Japan. Light Novel is a term used to refer to novels specifically written by Japanese writers. Light Novels are divided into multiple volumes, each volume printed as a separate book. The first Light Novel I read was "Yahari Ore no Seishun Rabu Kome wa machigatteiru." which translates to English as "My Youth Romantic Comedy is Wrong, as I Expected". It does have romance and comedy as its elements, but they're second to the "Slice of Life" genre. (You probably haven't heard the name of this genre, but don't worry too much about it. The name is pretty much self-explanatory.) It has a total of 14 volumes, which amount to approximately 4000 pages. One of the best points of Oregairu (it's short name) is that it never gets boring, especially if philosophy is an interest for the reader.
